üîç Why Does This Happen?

1. The Detour Problem

When Sarah picks up 4 passengers, she's likely:

  • Driving in opposite direction from the event to get passenger 1
  • Zigzagging across town for passengers 2 and 3
  • Making a final detour for passenger 4
  • Finally heading to the event

Her route might be 3x her direct distance!

2. Geographic Distribution

Your participants are likely spread out, not conveniently lined up along a single route. This creates a "traveling salesman" problem where picking up multiple people requires crisscrossing the area.

3. One-Way Trip Reality

Unlike commuting where you might carpool both ways, party carpooling is one-way. The detours to pick people up aren't offset by return trip savings.

4. Optimization Priority

The algorithm prioritizes minimizing vehicles over minimizing total miles because:

  • Fewer vehicles = less parking needed
  • Fewer vehicles = lower emissions overall
  • Fewer vehicles = reduced traffic congestion
  • The algorithm limits individual overhead to ~20 minutes max

  • Clustering: The 4 passengers might be relatively clustered near her route
  • Algorithm Efficiency: Using one vehicle for 5 people is better than using 2-3 vehicles

üîß How the Algorithm Handles This

The algorithm tries to balance several factors:

  1. Primary Goal: Minimize vehicles needed
  2. Secondary Goal: Limit individual overhead (max ~20 minutes per driver)
  3. Consideration: Geographic proximity for passenger assignments
  4. Result: Some drivers (like Sarah) do more work, but within reasonable limits

If the algorithm tried to minimize total miles instead:

  • Everyone would drive themselves (0 added miles)
  • You'd need 11 parking spots
  • Maximum emissions and congestion
  • No environmental or social benefit

üí° Bottom Line

Your optimization is working perfectly! Adding 2.2 miles total while eliminating 6 vehicles is an excellent outcome. Sarah's willingness to pick up 4 passengers makes the entire system work efficiently.

Remember: The goal isn't to minimize total miles driven, but to minimize the number of vehicles while keeping individual burden reasonable. The slight increase in total miles is far outweighed by the benefits of having 55% fewer vehicles on the road.

  • Decreased parking infrastructure requirements

Your 2.2 added miles are negligible compared to these benefits!
